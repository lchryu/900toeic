# IELTS Study Plan From 4.0

Current profile:

- Vocabulary: fairly strong.
- Reading: relatively good.
- Speaking: around A1, weak fluency and short answers.
- Writing: around A1, weak sentence building, linking ideas, and paragraph writing.
- Realistic target: from 4.0 to 5.5 in 4-6 months if you study consistently. Reaching 6.0+ will need more time, especially for Writing and Speaking.

Core principles:

- Do not jump into full Writing/Speaking test practice too early.
- Turn passive vocabulary into sentences you can actually say and write.
- Produce output every day: speak in full sentences and write paragraphs.
- Use Reading to maintain your score and collect ideas/vocabulary for Speaking and Writing.

## Stage 1: Weeks 1-4

Goal: move beyond A1 in Speaking and Writing. You should be able to speak and write clear simple/compound sentences.

### Speaking

Do these 3 things every day:

- Read 10 short model sentences aloud.
- Say 10 sentences about yourself.
- Record a 1-minute answer to an IELTS Part 1 question.

Weekly topics:

- Week 1: yourself, family, study, work.
- Week 2: hobbies, food, movies, music.
- Week 3: home, city, transport.
- Week 4: friends, habits, technology.

Required sentence patterns:

```text
I usually...
I often...
I prefer A because...
I used to..., but now I...
One thing I like about it is...
I am interested in...
It depends on...
In my opinion,...
```

Task:

- Choose 5 Part 1 questions every day.
- Answer each question in 3-4 sentences.
- Do not worry about sounding impressive. Focus on clear meaning and fewer pauses.

Example:

```text
Do you like music?

Yes, I do. I usually listen to pop music when I work or study. It helps me relax and focus better. I prefer songs with simple melodies because they are easy to enjoy.
```

### Writing

Goal for the first 4 weeks: write correct sentences first. Do not focus on essays yet.

Every day:

- Write 10 simple sentences.
- Write 5 compound/complex sentences with because/and/but/so/although.
- Write 1 paragraph of 80-100 words.

Paragraph structure:

```text
Topic sentence.
Reason 1.
Example.
Reason 2.
Final sentence.
```

Practice prompts:

```text
Write about your favorite place in your city.
Write about why people use smartphones.
Write about a skill you want to learn.
```

Grammar to master:

- Present simple.
- Past simple.
- Future with will/going to.
- Comparatives.
- There is/There are.
- Clauses with because/although/when/if.

Do not study grammar randomly. Fix the grammar you actually use incorrectly.

### Listening

Listen for 20 minutes every day:

- Listen once for the main idea.
- Listen again and write down 5-10 sentences.
- Read the transcript.
- Listen one final time.

Suitable sources:

- BBC Learning English.
- VOA Learning English.
- IELTS Listening Section 1.

### Reading

Since your Reading is already decent, do not spend too much time here.

Every day:

- Read 1 short text.
- Highlight 5 phrases you can use in Speaking/Writing.
- Write 5 new sentences using those phrases.

## Stage 2: Weeks 5-8

Goal: answer Speaking Part 1/2 properly; write basic Task 1 reports and Task 2 body paragraphs.

### Speaking

Weekly schedule:

- Monday/Wednesday/Friday: Part 1, 10 questions each day.
- Tuesday/Thursday/Saturday: Part 2, 2 cue cards each day.
- Sunday: record an 8-10 minute mock test.

Part 2 structure:

```text
I want to talk about...
It happened...
The main reason I remember it is...
At first,...
After that,...
In the end,...
Overall, I felt...
```

When you run out of ideas:

- Talk about the person.
- Talk about the time.
- Talk about the place.
- Talk about your feelings.
- Explain why it was important.

Do not force difficult vocabulary. Correct and fluent sentences matter more.

### Writing Task 1

Weeks 5-6: learn line charts and bar charts.

Structure:

```text
Introduction: paraphrase the question.
Overview: 2 main trends/features.
Body 1: main data group.
Body 2: remaining data group.
```

Useful sentence patterns:

```text
The chart shows...
Overall, it is clear that...
The figure increased from A to B.
There was a decrease in...
By contrast,...
The highest figure was...
```

Weeks 7-8: learn tables, pie charts, processes, and maps at a basic recognition level.

Each week write:

- 3 Task 1 reports.
- Rewrite each report once after checking mistakes.

### Writing Task 2

You do not need to write a full essay every day yet.

Every day, write 1 body paragraph of 100-130 words.

Body paragraph structure:

```text
Main idea.
Explanation.
Example.
Result/mini conclusion.
```

Topics:

- Education.
- Technology.
- Environment.
- Health.
- Work.
- Crime.

## Stage 3: Weeks 9-12

Goal: push your practical band level toward 5.0-5.5.

### Speaking

Every week:

- 3 sessions for Part 1.
- 2 sessions for Part 2.
- 2 sessions for Part 3.
- 1 full mock test.

Part 3 structure:

```text
I think...
One reason is that...
For example,...
However,...
So overall,...
```

Targets:

- Part 1: answer in 3-4 sentences.
- Part 2: speak for at least 1 minute 30 seconds.
- Part 3: answer in 4-5 sentences with a reason and an example.

### Writing

Every week:

- 2 Task 1 reports.
- 2 Task 2 essays.
- 2 sessions for grammar/collocation correction.

Self-correction checklist:

- Did you miss s/es?
- Did you use the wrong tense?
- Does every sentence have a main verb?
- Are you repeating words too much?
- Do you have a specific example?
- Is the Task 1 overview clear?
- Does Task 2 answer the exact question?

### Listening/Reading

Every week:

- 2 Listening tests.
- 2 Reading tests.
- Log mistakes by question type, not just by score.

Example error log:

```text
Listening Section 2: wrong because I missed plural/singular forms.
Reading True/False/Not Given: wrong because I inferred too much.
```

## Stage 4: Weeks 13-16

Goal: controlled test practice and better score stability.

Every week:

- 1 full Listening test.
- 1 full Reading test.
- 2 Writing Task 1 reports.
- 2 Writing Task 2 essays.
- 2 Speaking mock tests.
- 1 session only for reviewing old mistakes.

Sample weekly schedule:

```text
Monday: Listening + Speaking Part 1
Tuesday: Writing Task 1 + correction
Wednesday: Reading + Speaking Part 2
Thursday: Writing Task 2 + correction
Friday: Listening + Speaking Part 3
Saturday: Full Writing or full Speaking mock
Sunday: Error review + light study
```

## Daily Speaking Practice Set

Ask yourself 5 questions every day:

```text
What do you usually do in your free time?
Do you prefer studying alone or with others?
What kind of food do you like?
Do you like your hometown?
How often do you use your phone?
```

Answer formula:

```text
Answer directly.
Give one reason.
Give one example.
Add one feeling/opinion.
```

Example:

```text
I prefer studying alone because I can focus better. For example, when I study English, I usually need a quiet room. If I study with friends, I often talk too much. So studying alone is more effective for me.
```

---

## Daily Writing Practice Set

Write 1 paragraph using this form:

```text
I think [main idea].
This is because [reason].
For example, [example].
As a result, [result].
Therefore, [final sentence].
```

Practice prompts:

```text
Why do people use social media?
Why is English important?
Should students use phones in class?
Why do many people like living in cities?
Is online learning good or bad?
```

After writing, correct it:

- Underline the main verb in each sentence.
- Check the tense.
- Check singular/plural nouns.
- Replace 2 repeated words.
- Read the whole paragraph aloud.

## How To Use The Vocabulary You Already Know

Do not only learn meanings. Each word needs 1 sentence you can actually use.

Examples:

```text
important -> English is important because it helps me find better jobs.
convenient -> Online shopping is convenient because people can buy things at home.
improve -> I want to improve my speaking skills.
environment -> People should protect the environment by using less plastic.
```

Every day, choose 10 words you already know and write 10 simple sentences.

The goal is not difficult vocabulary. The goal is usable vocabulary in speaking and writing.

## Progress Checkpoints

After 4 weeks:

- You can answer Part 1 without going silent for more than 5 seconds.
- You can write a 100-word paragraph with fewer serious grammar mistakes.
- Listening Section 1 feels easier.

After 8 weeks:

- You can speak for around 1 minute in Part 2.
- You can write a basic Task 1 report.
- You can write a Task 2 body paragraph.

After 12 weeks:

- You can complete a full Speaking mock test.
- You can write full Task 1 and Task 2 responses.
- You know your most common mistakes.

After 16 weeks:

- Take a mock test to estimate your real band.
- If Speaking/Writing are still below 5.0, continue another 4-8 weeks of output-focused practice.

## What Not To Do Right Now

- Do not study too many idioms.
- Do not force band-8 vocabulary while your basic sentences are still wrong.
- Do not write long essays every day without correction.
- Do not only watch videos without speaking/writing.
- Do not do endless practice tests before your Writing/Speaking foundation is ready.
